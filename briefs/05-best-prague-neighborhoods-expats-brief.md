### H2: Vinohrady (Prague 2) — The Classic Expat Choice (350 words)
- **Vibe**: Tree-lined streets, art nouveau architecture, high expat density
- **Best for**: Dual-income couples, relocation packages, anyone wanting the "authentic but comfortable" experience
- **Rent range**: 24,000–30,000 CZK/month for 1BR
- **Pros**: Walkable, Riegrovy sady park, Náměstí Míru, best restaurant scene in Prague
- **Cons**: Most expensive, competitive rental market, can feel like a bubble
- **Service availability**: Highest — most Tool Connect providers cover Vinohrady as primary zone
- **Image**: Vinohrady street scene, art nouveau building

### H2: Karlín (Prague 8) — The Up-and-Coming Tech Hub (300 words)
- **Vibe**: Modern lofts, riverside cafes, tech company offices
- **Best for**: Tech workers, young professionals aged 25–35
- **Rent range**: 22,000–27,000 CZK/month for 1BR
- **Pros**: New construction, great food scene, walkable riverfront
- **Cons**: Still gentrifying (some areas feel unfinished), fewer green spaces
- **Service availability**: Good — growing rapidly
- **Image**: Karlín riverfront, modern apartment exterior

### H2: Holešovice & Letná (Prague 7) — The Creative Quarter (300 words)
- **Vibe**: Industrial-to-loft conversions, galleries, DOX museum
- **Best for**: Creatives, digital nomads, anyone wanting edge with accessibility
- **Rent range**: 20,000–26,000 CZK/month for 1BR
- **Pros**: Large apartments for the price, green parks (Letenské sady), great tram connections
- **Cons**: Some areas still rough around the edges; fewer international schools
- **Service availability**: Good
- **Image**: Holešovice street art / Letenský zámeček

### H2: Žižkov (Prague 3) — The Budget-Friendly Option (300 words)
- **Vibe**: Bohemian, pub culture, TV tower, local character
- **Best for**: Budget-conscious expats, people who want to live like a local
- **Rent range**: 18,000–23,000 CZK/month for 1BR
- **Pros**: Cheapest of the central options, easy metro/tram, genuine neighbourhood feel
- **Cons**: Less polished, fewer English-speaking services, some streets still rough
- **Service availability**: Moderate — fewer Tool Connect providers; may need to source from Vinohrady/Žižkov overlap

### H2: Smíchov (Prague 5) — Best for Families (300 words)
- **Vibe**: Transport hub, large apartments, quieter streets
- **Best for**: Families, people needing more space, commuters
- **Rent range**: 19,000–26,000 CZK/month for 1BR (significantly cheaper per m²)
- **Pros**: Larger apartments, good schools, direct metro to centre
- **Cons**: Less walkable for daily life, fewer expat-specific amenities
- **Service availability**: Good
- **Image**: Smíchov riverside family walking

### FAQ Section (4 items)
1. Is Prague safe for expats? — Yes; Prague consistently ranks among the safest capitals in Europe with one of the lowest violent crime rates in the EU.
2. Can you find English-speaking service providers in all Prague districts? — Central districts (Prague 1–8) have good English-speaker density; outer districts less so.
3. Which Prague district is best for families with children? — Smíchov (Prague 5) and Vinohrady (Prague 2) have the best international school access.
4. How competitive is the Prague rental market in 2026? — Very; rental stock is tight due to short-term rental restrictions and rising demand. Budget 2–3 months lead time.

### Conclusion (120 words)
- Recommendation: Vinohrady for most expats; Karlín for tech workers; Žižkov for budget-conscious.
