### H2: Electrical vs Plumbing — Key Differences to Know (250 words)
- **Answer-first**: Electricians require a specialized license (elektrotechnická způsobilost); general handymen cannot do electrical work legally
- Cover: What jobs require a licensed electrician (anything involving rewiring, fuse boxes, outdoor circuits) vs what a handyman can do (light bulb fitting, minor fixture changes), same distinction for plumbing
- **Chart**: 2-column table — "Job: who can legally do it" (handyman vs licensed plumber/electrician)
- **Key stat**: Czech electrical installations must comply with ČSN 33 2000 (Czech electrical standard) — only certified elektrotechnik can sign off

- **Answer-first**: Most platforms offer 24/7 urgent bookings at a premium; expect 50–100% surcharge
- Cover: Difference between "urgent" and "emergency," how to diagnose severity (burst pipe = emergency, dripping tap = next-day), what to do while you wait (water shutoff valve location, fuse box isolation)
- **Image**: Emergency plumbing / water shutoff valve

### FAQ Section (4 items)
1. How much extra do I pay for an emergency trade call-out in Prague? — Typically 50–100% surcharge; confirm before agreeing.
2. Can a handyman fix my boiler in Prague? — No; boiler servicing requires a certified gas engineer (topenář) in Czech Republic.
3. How do I know if a tradesperson is licensed? — Ask for their živnostenský list number and verify at rzp.cz (Czech trade register, public).
4. What if a tradesperson damages my property? — They are legally liable under Czech law (§2913 Civil Code); document all damage immediately with photos.
