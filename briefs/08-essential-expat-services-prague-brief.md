### H2: Legal & Administrative Services (400 words)
Services covered:
1. **Immigration lawyer / visa specialist** — CZK 3,000–8,000 for standard applications (firms: MuradVisa, Nawi Europe)
2. **Digital Foreigner Account setup** — free via Ministry of Interior portal (launched Jan 2026)
3. **Apostille & certified translation** — CZK 300–600/page; needed for most official documents
4. **Czech accountant** — CZK 1,000–3,000/month for freelancers; essential if self-employed
5. **Tax advisor** — Annual filing support; budget CZK 3,000–6,000 for first filing
- **Image**: Official documents / desk workspace

### H2: Home Services (400 words)
Services covered:
6. **Verified handyman** — CZK 400–700/hr; first port of call for flat maintenance (→ see Brief 01)
7. **Licensed plumber** — CZK 600–900/hr; for anything involving pipes, radiators, boilers
8. **Licensed electrician** — CZK 700–1,000/hr; legally required for rewiring and fuse box work
9. **Home cleaner** — CZK 200–350/hr; most expats hire bi-weekly
10. **Moving company** — CZK 1,500–3,000 for a Prague flat move; book 2–3 weeks ahead
11. **Internet installer** — CETIN fiber fastest; O2 and Vodafone for bundled packages
- **Image**: Handyman at work / Prague apartment interior

### H2: Health & Wellness Services (400 words)
Services covered:
12. **English-speaking GP** — Canadian Medical, Medicover, Pro Clinic; expect CZK 1,000–2,000/visit private
13. **Dentist** — Prague costs 40–60% less than Western Europe; widely English-friendly
14. **Therapist / psychologist** — English-language therapy available; CZK 1,500–2,500/session
15. **Pharmacy** — All pharmacies (lékárna) stock EU-standard medication; most staff speak basic English
16. **Personal trainer** — CZK 600–1,000/hour; Prague has a strong fitness culture
- **Chart**: Bar chart — Private healthcare service costs in Prague vs UK equivalent (in CZK / £)

### H2: Transport & Practical City Services (300 words)
Services covered:
17. **Lítačka transit card** — Public transport pass; buy at DPP kiosks or online; CZK 550/month unlimited
18. **Car mechanic** — CZK 600–900/hr labour; English-speaking recommended for Vinohrady/Karlín area
19. **Bike repair** — Prague cycling culture is growing; expect CZK 300–600 for common repairs
20. **Parcel/courier service** — Zásilkovna (Packeta) is the most-used for expats; English app available

### H2: Work & Business Services (300 words)
Services covered:
21. **Business address / virtual office** — CZK 500–1,500/month; needed if self-employed (živnost)
22. **Czech bank for business** — Air Bank and Fio Banka most expat-friendly; English online banking
23. **Co-working space** — Locus, Opero, WeWork Prague; CZK 3,000–8,000/month hot desk
24. **Czech language tutor** — CZK 500–800/hr; survival Czech is essential for daily life (→ see Brief 07)

### H2: Community & Social Services (200 words)
Services covered:
25. **Expat community hubs** — InterNations Prague, Expats.cz, Prague Families Facebook group, Meetup.com Prague

### FAQ Section (4 items)
1. What's the most important service to set up in the first week in Prague? — Health insurance and address registration; without these you can't access most other services.
2. Are there English-language customer support lines in Prague? — Major banks (Air Bank, ČSOB), utilities, and telecom providers have English options; government services are Czech-only unless you book an interpreter.
3. How do I find expat-specific services without speaking Czech? — Use vetted English-language platforms (Tool Connect), the Expats.cz directory, and r/prague on Reddit.
4. Are service costs in Prague rising? — Yes; Czech inflation and low unemployment (3.0%) are pushing service prices up ~5–8% per year, but Prague remains significantly cheaper than Western Europe.
