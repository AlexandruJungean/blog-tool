### H2: Days 1–3 — Legal & Administrative Essentials (400 words)
Items:
1. Register your address at the local municipal office (ohlašovna)
2. Apply for your Foreigner Account on the new digital immigration portal (launched Jan 2026)
3. Confirm your residence permit status (EU: voluntary in 2026, mandatory from 2027)
4. Get a Czech SIM card (Vodafone, T-Mobile, O2 all offer English-language support)
- **Key stat**: Czech Republic launched fully digital immigration system Jan 1, 2026 (VisaHQ)
- **Image**: Person at government office / digital device showing online form

### H2: Days 4–7 — Money & Banking (350 words)
Items:
5. Open a Czech bank account (Air Bank and Moneta are most expat-friendly)
6. Set up a local payment method (Google Pay/Apple Pay work widely)
7. Understand CZK exchange — Prague's Cost of Living Index is 61.5 vs NYC=100
8. Register for Czech tax ID (DIČ) if self-employed
- **Chart**: Bar chart — monthly expat budget breakdown (rent, groceries, utilities, services, transport)

### H2: Days 7–14 — Health & Insurance (300 words)
Items:
9. Confirm health insurance covers Czech public hospitals
10. Register with a GP (praktický lékař) — list needed documents
11. Locate the nearest English-speaking private clinic (popular: Canadian Medical, Medicover)
12. Check dental care options — Prague costs ~40% less than Western Europe
- **Key stat**: Monthly health insurance for a self-employed expat: approx. 2,968 CZK (2026 minimum)

### H2: Days 7–14 — Setting Up Your Home (350 words)
Items:
13. Check utility contracts (electricity, gas, internet) — who is responsible, you or the landlord?
14. Buy or source furniture — IKEA Prague is in Zličín and Černý Most
15. Find a verified handyman for any repairs needed before you're fully settled
16. Set up internet — CETIN fiber is fastest in Prague; O2 and Vodafone for bundles
17. Find a cleaner — expect 200–350 CZK/hour for regular home cleaning
- **Internal link**: "Find a verified handyman" → link to Brief 01 / that post

### H2: Days 14–21 — Neighbourhood & Community (300 words)
Items:
18. Get a Lítačka transport card (Prague public transport pass)
19. Join the local expat community — Expats.cz, InterNations Prague, Facebook groups
20. Find an English-speaking Czech language tutor (fastest way to reduce daily friction)
21. Locate your nearest supermarket, pharmacy, and hardware store
- **Image**: Prague street, tram, neighbourhood life

### H2: Days 21–30 — Work & Long-Term Setup (300 words)
Items:
22. Register your employer or živnostenský list if self-employed
23. Set up a Czech email address or mailing address if needed for official correspondence
24. Review your lease for maintenance responsibilities
25. Book your first service providers through a verified platform — don't rely on unvetted Facebook recommendations
- **Key stat**: 10%+ of Prague's population is now foreign-born (Czech Statistical Office, 2025)

### FAQ Section
1. Do EU citizens need to register in Czech Republic? — Voluntary in 2026, mandatory from 2027 for stays >90 days.
2. How long does it take to open a bank account in Prague? — Air Bank and Moneta: same day with passport + proof of address.
3. Is Czech language needed for daily life in Prague? — Prague 1–3: English is widely spoken; outer districts less so.
4. What's the fastest way to find a reliable cleaner or handyman in Prague? — Use a verified marketplace rather than social media groups.
