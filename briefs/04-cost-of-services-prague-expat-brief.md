- **Answer-first**: 200–350 CZK/hour for regular cleaning; end-of-tenancy deep cleans 3,000–6,000 CZK
- Cover: Regular vs deep clean pricing, agency vs independent cleaner, what's included, tipping norms (not expected but appreciated)

### H2: Tradespeople — Plumbers, Electricians, Handymen (350 words)
- **Answer-first**: Handyman 400–700 CZK/hr; plumber 600–900 CZK/hr; electrician 700–1,000 CZK/hr
- Cover: Emergency call-out premium (typically 50–100% surcharge), weekday vs weekend rates, materials vs labour split, who supplies materials
- **Chart 3**: Horizontal bar chart — Hourly rates by trade (with min/max ranges): General handyman, Plumber, Electrician, Tiler, Painter/decorator, Carpenter
- **Key stat**: Czech unemployment 3.0% in 2026 — low supply of skilled trades pushing rates up

### H2: Tutors, Language Teachers & Instructors (250 words)
- **Answer-first**: Czech language tutor: 500–800 CZK/hour; personal trainer: 600–1,000 CZK/hour; music/art: 400–700 CZK/hour
- Cover: Online vs in-person rates, group vs private, what qualifications to look for
- **Image**: Tutor session, student and teacher

### H2: IT Support & Digital Services (200 words)
- **Answer-first**: IT support: 600–900 CZK/hour; website freelancer: 800–1,500 CZK/hour
- Cover: Onsite vs remote support, one-off fix vs retainer, English-language availability

### H2: How to Avoid Overpaying as an Expat (300 words)
- **Answer-first**: Get 3 quotes; use a vetted platform; never pay full cash upfront
- Cover: Common price inflation tactics used on expats (vague quote + "extras," call-out fee not mentioned upfront), how verified platforms protect against this, using written quotes
- **Image**: Person reviewing a quote on their phone

### FAQ Section (4 items)
1. Are prices in Prague negotiable? — For larger jobs yes; for hourly rates, not typically.
2. Should I pay in cash or card in Prague? — Card is preferred for traceability; insist on a receipt regardless.
3. Why are plumbers and electricians more expensive than handymen? — Licensed trades (plumber/electrician) require specialized certification under Czech law; general handymen do not.
4. How much should I budget for services monthly as an expat in Prague? — A single expat should budget 2,000–4,000 CZK/month for cleaning, ad-hoc repairs, and occasional specialist services.
